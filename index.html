Net-Zero Mixed-Use Highrise

A net-zero energy mixed-use highrise designed primarily for natural daylighting and ventilation. In addition to design work, our team carried out the theoretical calculations necessary to show that rooftop and parking lot PV panels would be sufficient to power this 40,000 sq. ft. tower.

  • Class: Advanced Energy Efficient Building Design
  • Date: June 2016

Net-Zero House

A net-zero energy house that uses its geometry to take advantage of the sun for heating in the winter months and in the early morning, and to block the sun during the summer months and in the afternoon.

  • Class: Energy Efficient (Small) Buildings
  • Date: March 2016

Spatial Sustainability in Chicago

An assessment of livability across the neighborhoods of Chicago. I use data about each neighborhood's walkability, intersection density, bikability, urban greenspace, and landmarks to assign an overall livability score to each neighborhood. The map reveals inequality across the city resulting from its current design.

  • Class: New Indicators of Well-Being and Sustainability
  • Date: August 2016

Exhibition Center

An exhibition center designed for Jasper Ridge, near Palo Alto, CA. The building is designed with two wings, separated by an atrium space. The building is characterized by its shading: horizontal shades shield the souther curtain walls from the summer sun, while in the glass atrium, operable overhead shades selectively let in sunlight when heating is required.

  • Class: Building Information Modeling Integration
  • Date: December 2015

Restaurant Lighting Study

A restaraunt lighting study in Chicago, IL in which we compared fenestration and interior lighting alternatives from two perspectives: energy and sustainability, and aesthetics and ambiance.

  • Class: Building Systems
  • Date: March 2016

Northwestern University Senior Capstone

In our senior capstone design project, I served as the architect and the project manager. We designed a stormwater management solution for the town of Winnetka, IL that made use of a tunnel running from the town to Lake Michigan. My task was to redesign the landscapes both at the start of the tunnel and at the end of the tunnel. I also coordinated an 8-person team of engineers working on the structure, environmental performance, and constructuability of our solution.

  • Class: Northwestern Senior Capstone Design
  • Date: June 2015

Northwestern University Dance Marathon

I served on the Executive Board as Productions Chair for NUDM 2015, a student-run philanthropy organization that culminates with an event involving over 1400 students. Dancers and committee members came together to dance for 30 hours straight and raised money and awareness for the Starlight Children's Foundation and the Evanston Commmunity Foundation .

We raised $1.13 million dollars in total, the second highest cash total in the NUDM's history. Our donation to Starlight has led to the construction of 11 new Starlight Sites in the Chicagoland area.

In my role as Productions Chair I led a team of 60 students in the design of the lighting and set within the tent, the creation of dozens of promotional videos, and the management of all 30 hours of activities -- planned down to the individual minute.
